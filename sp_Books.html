Favorite Books and Series

I love to read books when I have free time. I love to read YA (young adult) fiction, fantasy, science fiction, and anything about vampires (except Twilight). Below are a few of my favorite books/series. You can click on each picture to see the full synopsis of each book/series on Goodreads and read reviews.

Six of Crows Duology

The Six of Crows duology consists of Six of Crows and Crooked Kingdom. They are written by Leigh Bardugo. The world in these books is called GrishaVerse. Leigh Bardugo is also my top favorite author. The Six of Crows duology are YA fantasy books. This duology follows 6 main characters who go on a journey to do an impossible heist that will reward them with a lot of money if they succeed.

The Hate U Give

The Hate U Give by Angie Thomas is a YA contemporary that follows the main character Starr Carter. Starr witnesses her childhood friend get shot by a police officer. This book is one of my favorites because I love the family dynamics. I also love that this book is #ownvoices, meaning that the author is either the same ethnicity or has the same disorders as the main character. In this case, Angie Thomas ia african-american and so is the main character. This book is so important and teaches readers that you have a voice and it is important. The Hate U Give is being adapted into a movie and you can watch the trailer here.

Percy Jackson and the Olympians

Percy Jackson and the Olympians is a 5-book series by Rick Riordan. It is a middle-grade fantasy. The first book is titled The Lightning Thief. This series is heavily influenced by Greek mythology which is one of the many reasons it is one of my favorite series. Percy Jackson as a main character is funny and sassy. Fun fact: Rick Riordan wrote the Percy Jackson series for his son who also has dyslexia. Also, there are movies for the first 2-3 books. However, they are awful. I have not watched them because even Rick Riordan tells teachers not to show the movies to their students.
